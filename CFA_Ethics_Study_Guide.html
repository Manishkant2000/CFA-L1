Reading 89 – Ethics and Trust in the Investment Profession

LOS 89.a–h
Core Definitions

🔑 Ethics (LOS 89.a)

  • Ethics: Shared beliefs about good/bad behavior
  • Behavior following moral principles consistent with society's expectations
  • Conduct that improves outcomes for stakeholders
  • Stakeholders: clients, coworkers, employers, profession
  • Balances self-interest with impact on others

🔑 Code of Ethics (LOS 89.b)

  • Code of Ethics: Written set of moral principles
  • Communicates values, principles, expectations
  • May include rules requiring minimum ethical behavior
  • Profession: Group with specialized skills + code of ethics
  • Signals commitment to serve clients honestly

🔑 Professions (LOS 89.c)

  • Specialized expert knowledge
  • Code & standards for professional behavior
  • Regulatory body to enforce rules
  • Focus on client needs & service to society
  • Client interests first
  • Continuing education requirement
  • Trust via: expertise, ethical standards, monitoring, mentoring

⚠️ Need for High Standards (LOS 89.d)

  • Investment management = intangible product
  • Hard to evaluate quality → trust more critical
  • Entrusted with client wealth
  • Unethical behavior → damages clients, firms, society
  • Financial services = intermediary between savers & borrowers
  • Misallocation of capital reduces economic growth
  • Perception of extra risk → reduces investment

🔑 Professionalism (LOS 89.e)

  • Suitability standard: Match client return/risk needs to securities
  • Fiduciary standard: Act in best interests of client (higher)
  • Clients often lack knowledge about fee structures, securities, bias

🚫 Challenges to Ethics (LOS 89.f)

  • Overestimate own ethical quality
  • External/situational factors more important than internal traits
  • Social pressure, loyalty, peer pressure
  • Financial rewards / prestige
  • Rules-based compliance → "what can I do?" not "what should I do?"
  • Short-term focus over long-term

⚖️ Ethics vs Law (LOS 89.g)

  • Not all unethical = illegal; not all illegal = unethical
  • Whistleblowing: may be illegal but ethical
  • Civil disobedience: illegal but many consider ethical
  • Undisclosed recommendation of relative's firm: legal but unethical
  • Ethical principles = higher standard than laws
  • Laws often follow unethical behavior (reactive)
  • Key laws: Securities Act 1933, Glass-Steagall, SEC Act 1934, Sarbanes-Oxley (post-Enron/Worldcom), Dodd-Frank (post-2008)
  • New laws → opportunities for different unethical behavior
  • Ethical decisions require more judgment than legal compliance

🔄 Decision-Making Framework (LOS 89.h)

1
IDENTIFY
Facts, stakeholders, duties, ethical principles, conflicts
2
CONSIDER
Situational influences, guidance, alternative actions
3
DECIDE & ACT
Short & long-term effects
4
REFLECT
Was outcome as anticipated? Why/why not?
  • Seek guidance from mentor, colleagues, legal/compliance
  • Senior management support is critical
  • Reduces unanticipated ethical consequences
  • Goal: balance interests of stakeholders

Reading 90 – Code of Ethics & Standards of Professional Conduct

LOS 90.a–c
Professional Conduct Program (LOS 90.a)

Program Structure

  • Governed by CFA Institute Bylaws + Rules of Procedure
  • Principles: fairness + confidentiality
  • Board of Governors: overall responsibility
  • Disciplinary Review Committee: enforces Code & Standards

Triggers for Inquiry

  • Self-disclosure on annual Professional Conduct Statement (PCS)
  • Written complaints received
  • Public sources (media)
  • CFA exam proctor report
  • Analysis of exam materials / social media monitoring

Investigation Process

  • Request written explanation
  • Interview member/candidate & complainant
  • Collect documents & records

Outcomes / Sanctions

  • No disciplinary action
  • Cautionary letter
  • Discipline (member may accept or reject)
  • If rejected → Disciplinary Review Panel hearing
  • Sanctions: peer condemnation, CFA Program suspension
  • NO suspension of designation during inquiry
Six Components of the Code of Ethics (LOS 90.b)

Members & Candidates MUST:

  1. Act with integrity, competence, diligence, respect
  2. Place integrity of profession & client interests above personal interests
  3. Use reasonable care & independent judgment
  4. Practice & encourage ethical professional behavior
  5. Promote integrity & viability of global capital markets
  6. Maintain & improve professional competence

Reading 91 – Guidance for Standards I–VII (Detailed)

LOS 91.a–c | 23 subsections

⚠️ Exam tip: Know what VIOLATES each standard. "Reasonable," "adequate," "token" are terms open to interpretation — exam will make violations clear. Actions prohibited regardless of motive (except when INTENT is key, e.g., market manipulation).

Standard I – Professionalism

I(A) Knowledge of the Law

  • Comply with most strict of: local law / country law / CFA Standards
  • Must dissociate from violations (stop participating)
  • Strongly encouraged (not required) to report to CFA PCP
  • May need to resign in extreme cases
  • Keep records of violations when dissociating

❌ Violations

  • Participating in known violations
  • Assisting client violate laws

✅ Recommendations

  • Maintain law copies
  • Consult compliance/lawyer if unsure

I(B) Independence & Objectivity

  • No gifts/benefits that could compromise independence
  • Normal business entertainment = permitted
  • Gifts from clients = less risky than from other parties
  • Client gifts must be disclosed to employer (before if possible)
  • Issuer-paid research OK if: flat rate, not tied to conclusions, disclosed
  • Allocating IPO shares to personal accounts = violation
  • Token gift: no defined monetary amount (firm should set policy)
  • Business dinner = likely token; week in Aspen/Super Bowl tickets = likely NOT

I(C) Misrepresentation

  • No knowingly false statements re: analysis, recommendations
  • Plagiarism: using others' work without credit
  • Citation NOT required for data from recognized financial/statistical reporting services (e.g., U.S. Treasury)
  • OK to omit names of departed firm members (as long as not claiming it as solely own work)

❌ Violations

  • Guaranteeing specific returns
  • Presenting 3rd-party research as own
  • Misleading benchmark selection
  • Omitting accounts from performance data
  • False claims about capabilities

✅ OK

  • Using public data without citation
  • Omitting names of former colleagues

I(D) Misconduct & I(E) Competence

I(D) Misconduct:

  • No dishonesty, fraud, or deceit in professional activities
  • No acts reflecting adversely on professional reputation/integrity/competence
  • Civil disobedience arrests ≠ necessarily misconduct
  • Cannot use Standard against others for personal/political disputes
  • Neglecting due diligence = misconduct

I(E) Competence:

  • Maintain abilities, skills, knowledge for professional role
  • New role → develop required competence
  • Continuing education = recommended, not required
  • Negative outcomes ≠ lack of competence
Standard II – Integrity of Capital Markets

II(A) Material Nonpublic Information (MNI)

  • Material: would affect price OR reasonable investor would want it before deciding
  • Ambiguous effect on price → may NOT be material
  • Nonpublic: not yet made available to marketplace
  • Analyst conference call = NOT public disclosure
  • Selective disclosure = creates insider trading risk
  • Prohibition extends to: related mutual funds, swaps, options
  • Member must verify info is public before acting
  • Info for intended purpose (e.g., investment banking) = OK; other uses = NO
  • Mosaic theory: public info + non-material nonpublic info = NOT a violation

Firewall recommendations: compliance clearance, employee trade review, watch/restricted/rumor lists, restrict proprietary trading.

II(B) Market Manipulation

  • No practices distorting prices or inflating trading volume
  • Key element: intent to mislead
  • Member actions affecting prices without intent = OK
  • Spreading false info to affect prices = violation
  • Trades intended to mislead = violation
  • Wash trading / matched orders = violation
Standard III – Duties to Clients

III(A) Loyalty, Prudence & Care

  • Clients' interests above employer & self
  • Does NOT impose new fiduciary duty, but requires acting in best interest
  • Soft dollars/commissions: must benefit the client
  • Vote proxies in informed, responsible manner (not all proxies need to be voted)
  • For pension/trust: duty owed to beneficiaries, not hiring firm
  • "Client" may be the investing public
  • Submit itemized statements to clients at least quarterly

III(B) Fair Dealing

  • "Fairly" ≠ "equally" (timing differences acceptable)
  • Different service levels = OK if disclosed and not disadvantaging clients
  • All clients get fair opportunity to act on recommendations
  • Inform clients of changes before accepting orders
  • No taking advantage of position (e.g., taking oversubscribed IPO shares)
  • Firms: limit # of people knowing rec changes; shorten time to dissemination

III(C) Suitability

  • In advisory relationship: gather IPS (Investment Policy Statement)
  • Inquire: risk/return objectives, financial constraints, investment experience
  • Reassess/update information regularly
  • Judge suitability in context of total portfolio
  • Portfolio manager to mandate: only investments consistent with stated mandate
  • Unsolicited unsuitable trade: discuss with client before executing
  • Minimal risk impact: may proceed after client acknowledges
  • Material risk impact: may need to update IPS; in absence, may end relationship

III(D) Performance Presentation

  • Fair, accurate, complete presentation
  • No misstatement or misleading claims
  • Cannot imply past returns will repeat
  • Brief presentations: make detailed info available on request
  • Include terminated accounts in history
  • Present composite performance (not just single account)
  • Disclose model results, gross/net of fees
  • Maintain supporting data/records

III(E) Preservation of Confidentiality

  • Keep client info confidential (current, former, prospective)
  • Exceptions:
    1. Client involved in illegal activities
    2. Disclosure required by law
    3. Client permits disclosure
  • Sharing with CFA PCP = NOT a violation
  • Confidentiality extends to former clients
  • Must report illegal activities to proper authorities (not to investment bank, etc.)
Standard IV – Duties to Employers

IV(A) Loyalty

  • Act in employer's benefit; don't deprive of skills/abilities
  • Client interests still above employer
  • Independent contractors: abide by agreement terms
  • No requirement to put employer interests above family
  • While employed: cannot solicit employer's clients; no misappropriation of client lists/trade secrets
  • Records on any medium (home computer) = firm's property
  • After leaving: knowledge of former client names/existence = generally not confidential
  • Independent practice: full disclosure + written consent from employer required
  • Social media: follow employer's policies; separate personal/professional accounts (best practice)

IV(B) Additional Compensation Arrangements

  • Must get written consent from all parties before accepting
  • Written consent includes email
  • Additional compensation: bonus tied to future performance → written consent in advance
  • Gift: bonus for past performance → disclosure to employer (Standard I(B))
  • Compensation = direct + indirect + third-party benefits

IV(C) Responsibilities of Supervisors

  • Reasonable efforts to ensure subordinates comply with laws, Code & Standards
  • Adequate compliance system must meet: industry standards + regulatory requirements + Code
  • Bring inadequate compliance to management's attention
  • If no/inadequate compliance: decline supervisory responsibility in writing
  • If violation found: respond promptly + investigate + limit wrongdoer's activities
  • Don't commingle compliance procedures with code of ethics
Standard V – Investment Analysis, Recommendations & Actions

V(A) Diligence & Reasonable Basis

  • Diligence, independence, thoroughness in analysis
  • Reasonable & adequate basis from appropriate research
  • Consider before recommendation: economic conditions, firm financials, fund fees/history, model limitations, peer group comparisons
  • Using 3rd-party research: must review assumptions + evaluate objectivity (required, not just recommended)

V(B) Communication with Clients

  • Disclose: nature of services + costs
  • Disclose: basic format of investment process; promptly disclose changes
  • Disclose: significant limitations and risks
  • Distinguish fact from opinion
  • Applies to ALL communication forms (emails, calls, reports)
  • Quantitative model: must disclose limitations & assumptions
  • Liquidity: ability to exit without significant extra cost
  • Capacity: vehicle's ability to absorb investment without reducing returns
  • Communicate performance in terms of total returns
  • If euro strategy only shows appreciation scenarios: violation (must include depreciation)

V(C) Record Retention

  • Develop & maintain records supporting analysis, recommendations, communications
  • Records = firm's property
  • All communications (emails, texts) = records to retain
  • Changed firms: re-create documentation from public info; cannot rely on memory or prior firm's materials
  • Recommended minimum: 7 years (if no regulatory guidance)
Standard VI – Conflicts of Interest

VI(A) Avoid or Disclose Conflicts

  • Avoid or make full and fair disclosure
  • Disclosures: prominent, plain language, effectively communicated
  • Most common: stock ownership in recommended companies
  • Compensation/bonus structure = common conflict
  • Board service = area of potential conflict
  • Disclose: broker-dealer market-making activities
  • Any compensation changes must be updated

VI(B) Priority of Transactions

  • Priority: Clients → Employers → Personal
  • Personal = where member is beneficial owner
  • Personal trades only after clients had adequate opportunity
  • Family accounts = treat like any client (no disadvantage, no preference)
  • Cannot act on pending trade info for personal gain (front-running)
  • Firms: blackout/restricted periods; duplicate trade confirmations; preclearance

VI(C) Referral Fees

  • Disclose any compensation received/paid for referrals to: employer, clients, prospects
  • All types of consideration must be disclosed
  • Must disclose even if research quality is good
  • Allows clients to evaluate full cost & potential partiality
  • Provide employer updates at least quarterly
Standard VII – Responsibilities as CFA Member/Candidate

VII(A) Conduct as CFA Program Participants

  • No cheating on CFA exam
  • No revealing broad OR specific topics tested, questions, formulas required/not required
  • No giving confidential CFA info to public
  • No improper use of designation for personal goals
  • No misrepresenting info on PCS
  • Violating CFA exam policies (calculator, belongings, Candidate Pledge) = violation
  • Members CAN express opinions about CFA Institute policies/procedures
  • Members CANNOT reveal exam content (even what was NOT tested)
  • Volunteers: cannot solicit/reveal exam question info or grading info

VII(B) Reference to CFA Designation

  • No misrepresenting or exaggerating meaning of CFA designation
  • No promotional promises/guarantees tied to CFA (e.g., "will achieve better returns")
  • Maintain membership: sign PCS annually + pay dues annually
  • No partial CFA designation
  • Acceptable: "I passed in 3 years" (if true)
  • Unacceptable: "I passed in 3 years therefore I'm superior"
  • If dues unpaid: no longer active; cannot use designation

Reading 92 – Introduction to GIPS

LOS 92.a–e

Why GIPS Were Created (LOS 92.a)

  • GIPS = Global Investment Performance Standards
  • Problem: firms chose self-serving methodologies
  • Biases: top portfolio cherry-picking, excluding terminated accounts, selecting favorable time periods
  • GIPS → standardized, comparable performance reporting
  • Benefits: clients, prospects, investors, oversight bodies

Who Uses GIPS (LOS 92.a)

  • Applies ONLY to firms managing assets for others
  • Compliance is voluntary
  • Must comply fully and on a firmwide basis to claim compliance
  • Software developers etc.: may endorse GIPS but cannot claim compliance
  • No partial compliance

8 Sections of GIPS (LOS 92.b)

  1. Fundamentals of Compliance
  2. Input Data & Calculation Methodology
  3. Composite & Pooled Fund Maintenance
  4. Composite Time-Weighted Return Report
  5. Composite Money-Weighted Return Report
  6. Pooled Fund Time-Weighted Return Report
  7. Pooled Fund Money-Weighted Return Report
  8. GIPS Advertising Guidelines

Composites (LOS 92.c)

  • Composite: grouping of individual discretionary portfolios with similar strategy/objective/mandate
  • Examples: large-cap stocks, investment-grade domestic bonds, index-matching
  • Must include all fee-paying discretionary portfolios (current AND past)
  • Must include terminated accounts
  • Firm assigns portfolio to composite before performance is known (ex ante)
  • Composite returns = asset-weighted average (NOT simple average)
  • Pooled funds must be included in a composite if they fit
  • Every fee-paying discretionary portfolio → at least one composite

Fundamentals of Compliance (LOS 92.d)

  • Firm = distinct business entity held out to clients
  • Multiple geographic locations under same name = all included
  • Discretion: firm's criteria for which portfolios are included
  • Nondiscretionary: client restrictions prevent manager from implementing intended strategy → may exclude from composite
  • Must provide GIPS-compliant reports to all clients/prospects
  • Info must be neither false nor misleading

Independent Verification (LOS 92.e)

  • Firms are encouraged (not required) to pursue verification
  • Applies to entire firm, not single composite
  • Must be performed by third party (not firm itself)
  • Verifier attests:
    1. Firm complied with GIPS composite construction firmwide
    2. Firm's processes established per GIPS calculation, data, & format requirements
  • Verified firms include standard disclosure language with name + dates + verifier

Decision Shortcuts for Application Qs

🎯 When in Doubt – Ask:

  • Whose interests are affected? → Clients first, then employer, then self
  • Is there disclosure? → Many issues resolved by timely disclosure
  • Is it the strictest rule? → Follow strictest of local/country/CFA
  • Is there intent to mislead? → Market manipulation requires intent
  • Is it public info? → Limited audience ≠ public
  • Is there written consent? → Additional compensation/independent work needs it

⚡ High-Frequency Exam Traps

  • Long-standing client / board relationship → does NOT excuse due diligence
  • Volunteer role + any compensation (parking) → still needs disclosure + consent
  • Civil disobedience ≠ misconduct violation
  • Chat room release ≠ public disclosure
  • Analyst conference call ≠ public disclosure
  • Pension manager's duty → beneficiaries (not hiring company)
  • Sharing with CFA PCP → never a confidentiality violation
  • Family accounts → treat like any client (no preference AND no disadvantage)
  • Passing CFA quickly ≠ implies superior performance
  • Tax advantages do NOT override suitability
